Ravi Kishan is an Indian actor and politician, who is a member of the parliament. He is known to have acted in several popular Hindi films such as Tere Naam (2003), Phir Hera Pheri (2006), and Welcome to Sajjanpur (2008). He made headlines in March 2023 after he revealed that he faced the casting couch during the early days of his career.

Wiki/Biography

Ravi Kishan Shukla was born on Thursday, 17 July 1969 (age 54 years; as of 2023) in Santacruz, Bombay (now Mumbai), Maharashtra, India. His zodiac sign is Cancer. According to some sources, Ravi Kishan passed his 12th standard at Rizvi College of Arts, Science & Commerce in Mumbai; however, according to other sources, he earned a bachelor’s degree in commerce at Rizvi College of Arts, Science & Commerce in Mumbai. [1]Lok Sabha [2]MyNeta When Ravi was ten years old, he along with his family shifted to Jaunpur in Uttar Pradesh, where he lived for seven years and helped his family in farming. While giving an interview, Ravi talked about it and said,

I stayed in Jaunpur for seven years and was simply not interested in studying. We just had some land on which we would do farming and were very poor. We lived in a mud house and could not even afford a sari for my mother on Diwali. I hated the thought of not being in Mumbai and felt I would either die or turn mad or become a goonda.”

At the age of seventeen, Ravi ran away from his home in Jaunpur and came back to Mumbai to pursue a career in acting.

Career

Actor

Hindi Films

Ravi Kishan made his acting debut with the 1992 film Pitambar, which was a B-grade film, starring Mithun Chakraborty, Raza Murad, and Kiran Kumar. Ravi received Rs. 5,000 as payment for acting in the film. Ravi talked about it in an interview and said,

I ran away from home to come to Mumbai. I then stayed in our old 10×12 sq ft place and met an old friend Hriday Shetty, who introduced me to a filmmaker. I got my first B-grade film Pitambar and made my first 5000. I bought a bike and started struggling.”

In 1994, he appeared in several Hindi films including Zakhmi Dil, Aag Aur Chingari, and Udhaar Ki Zindagi. He gained recognition after he shared the screen with Salman Khan in the 2003 film Tere Naam in which he played the role of Rameshwar. While talking about his role in the film, Ravi said that he tried to replicate his father’s look since he was a priest as well.

Ravi Kishan in a still from Tere Naam

He appeared in the 2004 film Aan: Men at Work as Raghu Shetty. In 2006, he played the role of a gangster named Chhote in the film Phir Hera Pheri.

Ravi Kishan as Chhote in Phir Hera Pheri

In 2007, he appeared as Captain Jacob in the film 1971. In the 2008 comedy film Welcome to Sajjanpur, Ravi Kishan played the role of Ram Kumar. He landed the role of Raghav Raghuvaran in the 2009 film Luck. In 2011, he shared the screen with R. Madhavan and Kangana Ranaut in the film Tanu Weds Manu. He bagged the role of Rajan in the 2012 film Agent Vinod. In 2013, he appeared in several Hindi films including Bajatey Raho as Mohanlal Sabharwal, Issaq as Teeta Singh (Tybalt), and Mere Dad Ki Maruti as Pathan. He landed the role of Sanjay Kumar in the 2017 film Mukkabaaz. In 2019, he played the role of an inspector named Krishan Kumar “K.K.” Verma in Batla House.

A photo of Ravi Kishan with John Abraham on the sets of Batla House

In 2021, he was signed up for the films Boondi Raita and Dehati Disco. In 2022, he landed a role in the film Love You Loktantra.

Bhojpuri Films

In an interview, Ravi Kishan said that he received an offer to work in a Bhojpuri film while he was struggling to gain recognition in Bollywood. Talking about it, he said,

There was a time when I was not making money in Bollywood and I was then offered a Bhojpuri film. I called my mom. We speak Awadhi at home that is close to Bhojpuri. She said, ‘Do it for your village people.’ I did it and became a Bhojpuri superstar and that changed my life.”

In 2003, he landed a role in the film Saiyaan Hamar. In 2004, he appeared in Pandit Ji Batai Na Biyah Kab Hoi. He played the role of Shankar in the film Kab Hoi Milanwa Hamar, which was released in the same year. In 2006, Ravi Kishan landed a role in several Bhojpuri films including Ab Ta Banja Sajanwa Hamaar and Ganga. In 2007, he acted in Banke Biharee M.L.A. He bagged a role in the 2009 Bhojpuri film Balidaan.

In 2011, he appeared in Hamaar Devdas, which was a remake of the Hindi film Devdas. In 2012, he acted in Kaisan Piyawa Ke Charitar Ba and Prem Vidrohi.

Politics

Ravi Kishan joined the Indian National Congress (INC) in 2014 and contested the Lok Sabha elections from the Jaunpur constituency in Uttar Pradesh, securing 42,759 votes. He, however, lost the elections. Ravi left the INC in 2017 and became a member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP).

A photo of Ravi Kishan with Amit Shah and Manoj Tiwari taken when he was joining the BJP

While giving an interview, he talked about the reasons for leaving Congress and said that after he lost the 2014 general elections, he felt dejected as no leader of Congress talked to him. He said,

It was a mistake. When I lost the election, no one in the party high command got in touch with me. I was dejected.”

In 2019, Ravi Kishan contested the Lok Sabha elections from the Gorakhpur constituency and secured 7,17,122 votes. In the elections, he defeated Rambhual Nishad, his nearest rival from Samajwadi Party, by a margin of 3,01,664 votes. Ravi said that he contested the elections on Yogi Adityanath’s seat in Gorakhpur after Prime Minister Narendra Modi and UP Chief Minister Yogi persuaded him to do so.

Ravi Kishan taking a selfie during the 2019 Lok Sabha elections campaigning

On 13 September 2019, he was appointed to the Standing Committee on External Affairs as its member; he held the membership till 12 September 2020. He became a member of the Standing Committee on Industry and Consultative Committee of the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways on 13 September 2019. He became the Permanent Special Invitee, Ministry of Power and Ministry of New and Renewable Energy on 13 September 2019. Ravi Kishan has always voiced his opinion in favour of framing a population control bill. While giving an interview in 2022, Ravi Kishan targeted Congress and said that he would not have had four children if Congress had brought the Population Control Bill. Talking about it, he said,

It is true that I have four children. So I know the struggle of raising them. I tasted success after a lot of struggle. Initially, we were asked to choose either work or money. My wife was tall and slim and after the first and second delivery, I saw her health deteriorating. I was struggling so at that time and used to remain always busy with shooting. Bachhe paida hote gaye. Had no clarity at that time. But now that I have achieved success and wealth when I look at my wife, I feel sorry.”

Controversy

A disparity in the electoral affidavit

In 2019, before the Lok Sabha elections, a youth named Santosh Kumar from Kushinagar complained to the returning officer against Ravi Kishan regarding a discrepancy in Ravi’s affidavit. In his complaint, the youth said that according to Ravi Kishan’s 2014 general elections affidavit, he held a bachelor’s degree in commerce; however, according to his 2019 affidavit, he was 12th pass. He wrote,

If Ravi Kishan was a commerce graduate in 2014, how did his educational qualifications come down to intermediate in 2019.”

Later, Santosh Mani Tripathi, the general secretary of the Rashtriya Mazdoor Congress, said that Ravi had written his name unclearly, moreover, his signature on the affidavit was missing as well. [7]India.com
