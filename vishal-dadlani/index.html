Vishal Dadlani is an Indian singer, music director, and lyricist. He works with his ‘partner in creation’ Shekhar Ravjiani, and they are popularly known as Vishal-Shekhar. Besides working in the mainstream Bollywood music industry, he is also a member of a rock/electronica band called Pentagram. He has also sung songs in Tamil, Telegu, and Marathi languages.

Wiki/Biography

Vishal Dadlani was born on Thursday, 28 June 1973 (age 50 years; as of 2023) in Bandra, West Bombay, Maharashtra. He grew up in Bandra, Mumbai, where he did his schooling.

Vishal Dadlani’s childhood photo

After completing his schooling at Hill Grange High School in South Bombay, he joined Jai Hind College, the University of Mumbai for two years, and afterwards, he went on to complete his graduation from H.R. College of Commerce and Economics, HSNC University. He received a bachelor’s degree in commerce in 1994. Vishal developed a sense of music when he listened to the music that his parents used to listen to. From Led Zeppelin to Jagjit Singh, he tried different languages and genres of music and experimented with them. The beginning of his career took place in 1994 when he started working as a lead vocalist in a band named Pentagram. After a while, he stepped into the music industry of Hindi cinema, and since then, there was no turning back.

Career

Pentagram (Band)

Pentagram is an Indian rock band that was formed in 1994, and Vishal is the lead singer in the band. It consists of three more members in which Shiraz Bhattacharya plays the drum, Randolph Correia is on guitar and Papal Mane is on bass. They are considered to be the forerunner in the field of independent music bands. Their debut album, We Are Not Listening, was released in 1996 and renowned poet and lyricist Javed Akhtar and music artist Shankar Mahadevan were featured in his album. After its debut, the band became very popular and released more albums such as ‘Up,’ ‘It’s OK, It’s All Good,’ and ‘Bloodywood.’ He rose to fame as a singer, composer, and lyricist after grabbing many Bollywood projects like Jhankaar Beats (2003), Bluffmaster (2005), and Salaam Namaste (2005).

Vishal-Shekhar: The Dynamic Duo

The duo was formed in 1999, and they went on to compose music for many popular films including Om Shanti Om (2007), Ra. One (2011), Student of The Year (2012), and Chennai Express (2013) The duo is counted among the best musicians in the Indian music industry.

Vishal-Shekhar (musical duo)

The Big Break in Bollywood

Shankar-Ehsaan-Loy was the first who gave Vishal an opportunity to sing in Bollywood films. The first song in which he lent his voice was “Kiss of Love” from the Bollywood film ‘Jhoom Barabar Jhoom’ in 2007; however, it was the song ‘Dhoom Again’ from the film ‘Dhoom 2’ that was released earlier than ‘Kiss of Love.’ Vishal’s songs have a wide range from pop to rock and from classical to modern. He has recorded more than 100 tracks, and most of them are superhits including Kurban Hua, Dhan Te Nan, Malhari, Tu Meri, Swag Se Swagat, Bala, and many more.
